Glaucoma Eye Yoga: Poses in Perspective

Yoga health guru Gary Kraftsow advises, “I would like people to know that yoga is not about touching your toes!” Taking his advice to heart, yoga-like mindful attention to eye muscles through “visual yoga” can help combat ocular conditions.

Anyone is a candidate for glaucoma, but one of the major risk factors is age. Over time, unused muscles lose their tone, flexibility, and conditioning. The eye’s ability to focus and the curvature of the lens may be affected. Numerous muscles are responsible for our

  • Eye movements
  • Pupillary contractions and dilations
  • Cleansing the eye with fluid and tear flow
  • Blood tissue perfusion

Eye yoga movements contribute to healthy, supple, and accommodating ocular tissue.  It is a complete package of

  • Posture
  • Breathing
  • Meditation
  • Relaxation
  • Movement
  • Stretching

The stress reduction benefits of visual yoga are coming into focus. There is research showing that acute closed-angle glaucoma is affected by our psychological and emotional condition. Intraocular pressures rise as our bodies fight, or flight stress mechanism engages.

Try these visual yoga exercises to improve eye health:

Weightlifting for the eyelids is a method that strengthens the tiny musculature.  Start by closing both your eyes about 50% and you will begin to feel the lids tremor. Work at reducing or stopping the shaking by focusing your gaze outward on a stationary object in the distance for about 10 to 15 seconds.  Breathe deeply from the nose and relax the ocular muscles by closing your eyes entirely.  Breathe out through your mouth.

Points of view Begin by centering yourself and affixing your gaze on things at variable distances. Select something close by on which to focus. Then look up or change the direction of your gaze to an object in the distance; after a few moments transfer back to the nearby object. Repeat this about ten times.

Ocular stretches build up the muscles that move our eyes from side to side.  Begin by blinking to bathe your eye in natural lubricant. Breathe and relax, sitting with good posture in a supportive chair. Keep your head stationary and move just your eye to the right and hold to a count of five. There will be a slight tension in your eye muscle.  Relax, blink and breathe. Repeat by shifting your gaze in the opposite direction repeating the process. Do this again three times then stop.

Bull’s eye target stretches continue to work the ocular muscles. Relax, breathe, and visualize a bull’s eye target. Using your eyes start someplace on the circle and follow the circumference around. Gently and slowly, complete a cycle of three in one direction than rest. Relax, breathe, and repeat back the other way.

As always, start slow and practice. If touching your eyes is involved, wash your hands at all times. Gradually build up to about 20 minutes every day.  Pull back and stop right away if you feel lightheaded or any pain or discomfort, and vision changes. Never stop your prescribed medications, using your vision correction glasses or contacts, and ocular health maintenance practices.  Reach out to your healthcare provider with any questions or concerns.
