Glaucoma Digital Apps

Lumos early glaucoma detection

WEBSITE: http://www.lumosvision.com/

APP STORE: N/A

GOOGLE PLAY: N/A

Function: Lumos early glaucoma detection is a mobile application in development that screens the eye for glaucoma.

Background: This app uses glaucoma pattern recognition from a vast digital reference library and breaks apart ocular anatomy after capturing a digital image with a smartphone. The person then receives the pressure measurement outcome. The results are on the screen in real time. A lens attaches to the smartphone and takes a digital image of the eye. The cost of a lens will be about $30.  Lumos is geared for technology-savvy adults and is the next wave in wearable biosensors!

What It Does Best: Makes detecting glaucoma incredibly easy, which is critical because many people go undiagnosed until too late

Why It’s Popular: Real time results

Awards: Recipient of the Young Innovators to Watch Award as part of the Mobile Apps Showdown

The Drawback: Cost of the lens to attach to smartphones, not available to the general public.

Glaucoma from Wills Eye

WEBSITE: https://www.willseye.org/

ITUNES STORE: https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/glaucoma-from-wills-eye/id1099960071?mt=8

GOOGLE PLAY: N/A

Function: Educates users about glaucoma and treatment options.

Background: Wills Eye Hospital designed a useful, medical quality, specialty app for those who have glaucoma and other ocular conditions.  The application has educational videos, information on how to use eye drops, the benefits of glaucoma laser treatments and surgery, an eye-drop or medication reminder feature, storage of medical information, and data tracking of eye pressure results.

What It Does Best: For patients and their caregivers in a smartphone-based and tablet-based glaucoma application

Why It’s Popular: Medical quality specialty app for smartphone or tablet, iPhone, iPad

The Drawback: Currently unavailable for Android devices

Implantable Ocular Biosensor

WEBSITE: N/A

ITUNES STORE: N/A

GOOGLE PLAY: N/A

Function: Stanford University and Bar-IUlan University developed an eye implant that continuously monitors intraocular pressure.

Background: The device is an eye implant sensor placed directly in the eye. The glaucoma tracker works with a smartphone camera to constantly self-monitor eye tensions.

The smartphone is equipped with an individual optical adaptor that generates a photo image which the app analyzes for glaucoma patterns, calculates the intraocular pressure, and stores the data. The tiny eye implant measures pressure much like a thermometer measures temperatures.

The implantable sensor is highly sensitive to consistent, reproducible data points. It is simple to use, does not change or distort vision, and allows for quick and efficient treatment modifications. This significant advancement in monitoring and subsequent rapid treatment to varying pressures minimizes retinal cells damage. They are featured by Allergan Glaucoma in Perspective and Triggerfish by Sensimed.

What It Does Best: Twenty-four/seven monitor records and reports to wearer at anytime from anywhere

Why It’s Popular: iOS and Android devices, accurate, updated treatment plan

The Drawback:  cutting edge technology seeking patients for ongoing clinical trials

Triggerfish by Sensimed

Website: http://www.sensimed.ch/en/sensimed-triggerfish/sensimed-triggerfish.html

APP STORE: N/A

GOOGLE PLAY: N/A

Background: Triggerfish by Sensimed captures spur-of-the-moment readings through a soft silicone contact lens. The wireless antenna positioned around the eye and the data records on a wearable device. Information transmits by Bluetooth to the physician's’ computer software.  Pending US FDA approval, the medical technology company is soliciting patients.

What It Does Best: Twenty-four/seven monitor records changes in ocular dimensions.

Why It’s Popular: Non-invasive system, records while sleeping and during daily activities

The Drawback: Outpatient installation and removal procedure by specially trained SENSIMED Triggerfish® system healthcare professional only.

Tonometer DIATON, Glaucoma IOP Eye Test

WEBSITE: http://www.TonometerDiaton.com

APP STORE: N/A

GOOGLE PLAY: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.conduit.app_7caf2d366c734cc5b0b7cee0e80ab6d7.app

Background: Tonometer DIATON, Glaucoma IOP Eye Test checks the reading of intraocular pressure without touching the cornea. So anesthesia eye drops are not needed. Therefore, the risk of corneal scratching and infection are at a minimum.   Rather, it utilizes the upper eyelid for contact. There is no calibration needed.

What It Does Best: Unique in its field, no extra purchases of covers or tips.

Why It’s Popular: free app, pen-like, a portable form that is comfortable to hold, and use, no sterilization required.

The Drawback:  Training and interpretation on the user end.
