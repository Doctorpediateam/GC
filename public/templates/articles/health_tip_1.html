Glaucoma Lifestyle Hacks

Glaucoma isn’t a curable condition, so a diagnosis requires some lifestyle changes. Here are some useful tips and habits to consider as adjunct remedies.

The first one out of the gate is quitting smoking. As we age, anything that helps reduce our overall health risks is on the table for consideration and choice. There is enough data that demonstrates the interconnectedness of age-related cataracts, macular degeneration and the progression of the diabetic ocular disease to the role of smoking. On the flip side, several credible research findings question the link and indicate that smoking does not affect glaucoma.

Does increasing your activity level affect glaucoma? Historically touted as good for the mind, body, and spirit, exercise is a standard prescription. Ongoing research is supporting a relationship between higher physical activity and long-term favorable effects on blood flow to the eye, referred to as ocular perfusion pressure (OPP). Enhanced or improved blood flow delivers oxygen and nutrients to the tissues of the optic disc. As a side benefit, even mild exercise has cardiovascular paybacks!

Using a digital application medication reminder systems can help with remembering to apply eye drops. Also, you may be taking several other medications for concomitant diseases that can be managed through the system as well. Linking your medication dosing time to another daily activity, creating a check off-list, or setting alarm clocks are other favorable options.

Did you get all of the medicated drops into your eye? Each drop of the medication is calibrated to deliver more volume than the eye can handle. Therefore, if you feel some gently roll down your cheek or spill out from the eye sac, do not worry. There is plenty left to go around and absorb into the ocular system. Remember, in the case of medications, more is not better! It is unwise to administer additional drops than prescribed. Besides, you will be throwing away and wasting the drug.

Generic medications come out all the time, as proprietary drug patents expire. Reach out to your pharmacist. They are in the best position to let you know if there is a cost-effective generic available. Even if your prescription health insurance covers your medication, if possible, reducing the copay per order can help you stretch your valuable dollars. Also, some people have difficulty squeezing those tiny eye drop bottles. Your pharmacist is a great position to make alternative recommendations.
